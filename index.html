Condition Explanations
Pattern: this sequence represents the numbers to pick from pre-determined groups of numbers where the criteria is how many draws ago a certain number appeared. For example, for Lotto Max, a pattern of 1-6-0 means the generated sequences will have 1 number that appeared on the last draw, 6 from 2 to 12 draws ago, and 0 from older draws. For the Lotto 6/49, numbers are picked from the last draw, draws 2 to 13 ago, and older draws. For the Daily Grand, numbers are picked from the last 6 draws, draws 7 to 14 ago, and older draws. Historical data shows that some of these patterns appear more often than others, look at the statistics section for more information, and to generate the best lottery numbers.

Miss 10s: this condition means that a group of tens will not be picked for a given sequence. For example, if the condition is Yes, then there could be no numbers picked from 1 to 9, or 20 to 29. Any group group of tens could be missed. In most cases, lottery history suggests that at least one group of tens is missed in the winning numbers sequence.

Even/Odd: this option allows you to choose how many even and odd numbers will appear on a given sequence. For example, 4/3 means there will be 4 even numbers and 3 odd numbers in all the sequences generated. Previous winning numbers are often evenly distributed.

Hidden: none of the sequences generated will have four or more consecutive numbers picked. Also, none of the sequences will have four or more numbers that have appeared together in more than five previous draws. Historical data shows that these cases are very rare, which is why these conditions are disabled.
